Clinically Proven: Why Heat Therapy Works

Heat therapy for menstrual pain isn't just folk wisdom — it's backed by rigorous clinical research. Here's what the science says:

clinical_notes

Continuous low-level topical heat (40°C/104°F) was shown to be as effective as ibuprofen (400mg) for relieving menstrual cramp pain, with faster onset and no side effects.

Akin, M. et al. (2001). “Continuous low-level topical heat in the treatment of dysmenorrhea.” Obstetrics & Gynecology, 97(3), 343–349. PubMed: 11239634

clinical_notes

Heat activates TRPV1 receptors in the skin, which block pain signals at the spinal cord level — the same mechanism as capsaicin cream, but without the burning sensation. Heat also increases blood flow to the uterus, reducing ischemia (oxygen deprivation) that causes cramping.

Abdel-Salam, O. (2014). “Capsaicin as a therapeutic molecule.” Progress in Drug Research, 68. & Proctor, M. & Farquhar, C. (2006). “Dysmenorrhoea.” BMJ Clinical Evidence. PubMed: 16973981

clinical_notes

Adding vibration to heat therapy further reduces pain intensity. A 2019 study found that combined heat + vibration produced significantly greater pain relief than heat alone for primary dysmenorrhea.

Lee, B. et al. (2019). “Effect of vibration on pain during local heat therapy in primary dysmenorrhea.” Journal of Physical Therapy Science, 31(2), 159–163.

verified

All citations are from peer-reviewed medical journals. Click the PubMed links to verify.
